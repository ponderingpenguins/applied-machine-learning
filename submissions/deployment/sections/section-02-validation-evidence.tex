\section{Validation Evidence}

We evaluate all three methods using equal error rate (EER), the standard metric for biometric verification. A random baseline achieves 50\% EER (accept or reject with equal error). Lower is better.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \begin{tabular}{lr}
    \toprule
    Method & EER \\
    \midrule
    Transformer & 7.68\% \\
    LSTM & 16.90\% \\
    FFT + Centroid & 49.54\% \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
  \caption{Equal error rates across three gait classification methods.}
\end{table}

The Transformer model substantially outperforms both baselines. The LSTM achieves moderate improvement over the hand-crafted FFT features, which perform near chance level. This progression reflects the ability of learned embeddings to capture discriminative gait patterns that simple frequency-domain features do not.
